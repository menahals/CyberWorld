Cyber Threats

Understanding the most common cyber threats is the first step to protecting yourself online. Cyber threats are malicious activities that target computer systems, networks and data which can cause significant damage to individuals and organizations.

Phishing

Fraudulent attempts made by attackers posing as trusted entities to trick people into revealing sensitive information.

Examples:

  • Fake emails from banks
  • Suspicious links in messages
  • Spoofed websites

Malware

Malicious software designed to harm or gain unauthorized access to systems.

Examples:

  • Viruses
  • Worms
  • Trojan horses

Ransomware

Malicious software that encrypts a user's data and demands payment for their release.

Examples:

  • File encryption attacks
  • System lockouts
  • Data hostage situations

Identity Theft

Unauthorized use of another person's personal information to commit fraud or other crimes.

Examples:

  • Stolen credit cards
  • Social security fraud
  • Account takeovers

Man-in-the-Middle Attacks

Unauthorised interception and modification of communication between two parties.

Examples:

  • Public WiFi attacks
  • Session hijacking
  • DNS spoofing

Social Engineering

Manipulation of individuals to gain confidential information or access to systems.

Examples:

  • Baiting
  • Tailgating
  • Impersonation

Stay Vigilant

Cyber threats are constantly evolving. Attackers use sophisticated techniques to exploit vulnerabilities in systems and human behavior. Always be cautious when:

  • Opening email attachments
  • Clicking on unknown links
  • Sharing personal information
  • Using public WiFi networks
